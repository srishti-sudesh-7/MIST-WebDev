My Favourite Albums of 2024

Let's look at the albums I enjoyed the most this past year!

Album Album Cover Artist Release Year Genre Note
The Dark Side of The Moon Pink Floyd 1973 Progressive Rock, Psychedelic Rock

A timeless classic blending progressive rock and psychedelic sounds. Its atmospheric production and introspective themes of mental health, human experience, and time create a deeply immersive, reflective experience that has stood the test of time.
CHROMAKOPIA Tyler, The Creator 2024 Alternative-Rap, Neo-Soul

A bold, genre-bending project blending psychedelic, jazz, and hip-hop influences. The production is vibrant and experimental, with Tyler’s introspective lyrics delving into themes of self-expression and growth. The eclectic soundscapes create a unique, immersive journey that showcases Tyler’s evolution as an artist.
Favourite Worst Nightmare Arctic Monkeys 2007 Indie Rock, Alternative Rock

A dynamic follow-up blending punk energy with refined melodic songwriting. The album captures youthful rebellion and introspection, featuring raw performances and standout tracks like “505” and “Brianstorm.”
Blonde Frank Ocean 2016 Psychedelic pop, R&B, Progressive soul

A deeply personal, genre-defying masterpiece blending R&B and soul. Its poetic lyricism and haunting production explore themes of love, longing, and self-discovery, creating a transcendent and modern classic.
Ultraviolence Lana Del Rey 2014 Indie Rock, Baroque Pop

A dark, cinematic exploration of toxic relationships and personal pain. With lush, haunting production and poetic lyricism, the album captures themes of obsession and heartbreak, blending vintage glamour with modern-day despair.
